EU Regulation 2023/1115 — EUDR

Every establishment. Whole of life. No minimum dwell time.

The EU Deforestation Regulation requires Australian feedlots and processors supplying EU beef markets to provide a complete record of every premises where cattle were kept — from birth property to kill floor — packaged as a verifiable Due Diligence Statement.

Whole-of-life establishment records. Every property the animal passed through — breeder, backgrounder, saleyard, feedlot, processor — captured automatically via NLIS integration.
WGS-84 geolocation per establishment. Coordinates ingested from the LPA Geolocation Tool for every PIC, validated to six decimal places, stored against the animal record.
Deforestation assessment per coordinate. Each establishment assessed against satellite imagery. Status cached and flagged for re-check when the retention window expires.
Signed DDS package per consignment. JSON + GeoJSON bundle with a human-readable PDF sidecar. Cryptographically sealed, timestamped, and retained for the full five-year regulatory window.
Pre-flight gap detection. Chain-of-custody checks and geolocation completeness verified before the package generates. Missing data surfaced and resolved before the kill, not after.

December 2026 application date. Australian feedlots and processors supplying EU markets need their due diligence systems operational before the first shipment under the new regime. BeefTrace is the operational layer that makes that possible — without adding headcount or manual process.
